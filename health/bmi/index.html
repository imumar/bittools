What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number calculated from a person's weight and height. It's a simple tool to broadly categorize weight status: underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

How is BMI calculated?

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m) × height (m)). For example, if you weigh 65 kg and are 170 cm tall (1.70 m), your BMI = 65 / (1.7 * 1.7) = 22.49.

FAQs about BMI

🔹 Is BMI a diagnostic tool?

No — BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure. It doesn't take account of muscle mass, bone density, overall body composition, and racial and sex differences.

🔹 What are the BMI categories?

- Underweight: < 18.5
- Normal weight: 18.5–24.9
- Overweight: 25–29.9
- Obesity: ≥ 30
